Abstract

With the popularity of the Internet, more and more applications and systems are being deployed on the Internet for people to use. However, leakage of user information occurs from time to time due to the system loopholes and network attacks. Therefore, more and more users are paying attention to the security, privacy and reliability of the online system. By solving users' pain points, it can attract more users to use eSystems.

I.INTRODUCTION

Nowadays, much more eSystems are used to complete our daily works. However, there are many reports about the leak of user info of some website and applications, which has deepened the user's distrust of the eSystems[1]. There was more than 50 million users of Facebook information has been leaked[2]. It is essential that improved the security of the eSystems and protected the privacy of end-users. This paper summarises previous researches about trust of users on eSystems and discussed how to use different methods to improve the end-users' acceptance of the eSystems.

II.FINDING/DISCUSSION

Naelah Al-Dabbous, Anwar Al-Yatama and Kassem Salehfinding pointed out eight aspects that affect the credibility of the eSystems, such as privacy, communication and information, technology, personal service and so on[1]. Among them, technology, privacy, communication and information are the areas where users pay the most attention. They designed some questionnaires to access end-users to obtain accurate information about some factors that affect the use of eSystems by end-users. Therefore, improving end-user trust in eSystems has become the main point.

One of the ways to improve end-user confidence is to strengthen the privacy protection of end-users, Johan Mazel, Richard Garnier, Kensuke Fukuda compared a series of online privacy protection technologies in their research. They believe that Third-party trackers are currently the most threatening user privacy. After analysing the current online privacy protection technologies, their research shows that Request Policy Continued and Noscript technology is the most effective privacy protection method at present[3]. But for end-users, it is still challenging to achieve good results through settings the protect plugins or applications.

Another measure to increase user trust is to enhance the security of the system. At present, Hypertext Transfer Protocol Security (HTTPS) is increasingly used. HTTPS uses SSL / TLS encryption to create a secure channel on the user side and server-side, which can prevent eavesdropping and man-in-the-middle (MITM) attacks. Richard Thomchick and Tonia San Nicolas-Rocca conducted relevant tests on the security of HTTPS in their research. They concluded that the combination of using HTTPS and restricting the use of cookies could significantly enhance the safety of the eSystems[4].

In my opinion, it is not enough to meet the functional needs of end-users but also needs to improve users' trust for eSystems is also another essential part of works. By increasing the privacy and security of eSystems through a variety of technical means such as HTTPS and Noscript technology, it is possible to increase the end-users' trust in eSystems, thereby attracting more users to use eSystems.

III.CONCLUSION

The purpose of this paper is to discuss how to enhance the security, privacy, and reliability of eSystems in conjunction with existing research. Using different kinds of technology to improve safety and protect user's privacy, we can improve user's acceptance of the eSystems so that we can let more and more users use eSystems. In other words, only by meeting the needs of end-users for security and privacy can we further deepen users' trust in the eSystems.
